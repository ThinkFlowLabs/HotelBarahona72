# Booking Channels & OTA Economics — Hotel Barahona 72

**Current state:** No website. Listed on OTAs (Booking.com) and via **Ayenda** (as "Ayenda 1313 Barahona 72"). Strong **Google Business Profile with ~430 reviews**. Rates ~USD 35–60/night, mostly domestic guests.

---

## 1. OTA dependence & commission economics

Independent budget hotels in LatAm commonly source **more than half of online bookings through OTAs** because they lack the marketing scale and brand recognition of chains. Commissions quietly erode already-thin budget margins.

| Channel | Typical commission to hotel | Notes |
|---------|----------------------------|-------|
| **Booking.com** | **~15% base, 10–25% range** (higher with "Preferred Partner"/visibility programs) | Agency model: hotel sets rate, commission charged after checkout; cancelled stays = no commission. Dominant for domestic Colombian travelers. |
| **Expedia Group** (Expedia, Hotels.com) | **~15–25%** (agency or merchant/net-rate) | Less central for domestic guests; matters more for North American/regional. Reaches hotel via Ayenda feed too. |
| **Ayenda** | **16% per reservation** on Ayenda-channel bookings | Asset-light "franchise by conversion" model (since May 2023): gives the hotel a reservation system, room-blocking, channel management, OTA connectivity, and the recognizable Ayenda budget brand. Core tool free; advanced features paid. Positioned as charging "only when the hotel generates profit." |
| **Direct (phone / WhatsApp / walk-in)** | **~0%** (only bank-transfer/card processing cost) | The commission-free goal. |

**What this costs in cash:** On a USD 50 room, 15–25% OTA commission = **USD 7.50–12.50/night**; Ayenda's 16% = **USD 8/night**.

**Worked example (illustrative):** 600 room-nights/month at USD 50 = USD 30,000 gross. If 80% (480 nights) sell via OTA at avg 18% commission = **USD 4,320/month in commissions**. Converting just **15% of those to direct** saves **~USD 648/month ≈ USD 7,800/year** — real money for a budget property.

**Ayenda nuance to clarify in the contract:** Ayenda's 16% generally applies to bookings *originating on Ayenda's own channel*. When Ayenda feeds an external OTA, the booking may carry both Ayenda's fee and the OTA commission — so "Ayenda bookings" are semi-direct but still commission-bearing. Direct phone/WhatsApp is the only truly commission-free path.

**Rate parity caveat:** OTAs discourage publicly undercutting their rates. Direct-booking advantages should be delivered as **value-adds** (late checkout, small upgrade, welcome perk, flexible cancellation) rather than a lower public price.

## 2. WhatsApp & direct phone — the domestic booking backbone

- WhatsApp is the **default commercial communication layer** in Colombia; travelers list it among essential trip apps. Domestic guests are comfortable confirming a booking by chat and paying via bank transfer or cash on arrival.
- **Open rate ~95%** for WhatsApp vs ~20–25% email (Journeytok hotel data).
- Many Colombian budget hotels **already take bookings informally over WhatsApp**; the opportunity is to **formalize and speed it up**:
  - WhatsApp Business profile (name, address, hours, description).
  - Automatic greeting + **quick replies** for the 5 core questions: availability, price, amenities, location, cancellation.
  - **Labels** to track the funnel (New Inquiry / High Intent / Awaiting Payment / Confirmed / Follow-Up).
  - **Follow-up rules** (~30 min and ~4 hrs after a quote) to recover bookings that would otherwise drift to an OTA.
  - Track weekly: inquiries → confirmed direct bookings.
- **Phone** still converts well (immediate reassurance) — keep the GBP/OTA phone number accurate and answered during booking hours; encourage callers to continue on WhatsApp for written confirmation, maps, and payment instructions.

## 3. Google Hotels, Google Business Profile & "Free Booking Links"

Google's hotel ecosystem = **GBP** (the listing) + **Google Hotels** (metasearch module in Search/Maps) + **Hotel Ads (paid)** and **Free Booking Links (unpaid)**.

- **Free Booking Links** let a hotel's own booking page appear next to OTA rates at **no click cost** — but require (a) a real **booking-page URL on the hotel's own site** (Google explicitly forbids an OTA or Facebook URL) and (b) **an integration partner** to feed rates/availability. **Self-service Google rate management ended 30 July 2025**, so a connectivity partner (CRS/booking engine) is now required.
- **Consequence for a hotel with NO website + no booking engine:** it **cannot** appear as a *direct* option in Free Booking Links. In Google Hotels it will only show **OTA/Ayenda rates** (commission-bearing).
- **BUT** Google Business Profile still gives two near-zero-cost direct gateways:
  - **"Call" button** — one-tap dial from Search/Maps on mobile.
  - **WhatsApp chat button** — a free, mobile-only GBP feature (Contact → Chat → WhatsApp, add `https://wa.me/NUMBER`). Appears beside Call/Directions/Website and drops the user straight into the hotel's WhatsApp. **This effectively becomes the hotel's "direct booking link" without a website.**

So a website-less hotel can absolutely win direct bookings through Google — just via **call + WhatsApp**, not Free Booking Links.

## 4. Metasearch (Trivago / Kayak / TripAdvisor / Google Hotels) — does it matter here?

- Metasearch is growing (market ~USD 4.8B in 2025 → ~USD 10.1B by 2034) but mostly **shifts demand between channels rather than creating new demand**.
- For a **budget hotel in a secondary, domestic-leaning city like Barranquilla**, direct metasearch participation isn't feasible without a booking engine, and most metasearch bookings will route through OTAs anyway (carrying commission).
- The one metasearch surface that matters is **Google Hotels**, because it's woven into normal Google search behavior. **Action:** keep NAP/photos correct so the hotel renders well in the Google Hotels module, but **do not invest in paid metasearch bidding** — it won't generate commission-free direct bookings here.

## 5. THE single biggest, fastest direct-booking opportunity

**Build a WhatsApp-centric direct-booking funnel anchored in the Google Business Profile.** This beats the alternatives on speed + cost + fit:

| Option | Speed | Cost | Direct-booking impact for THIS hotel |
|--------|-------|------|--------------------------------------|
| **WhatsApp button on GBP + optimized GBP + WhatsApp Business workflows** | **Minutes–days** | **~$0** | **Highest** — turns 430-review search visibility into commission-free chat bookings, matches Colombian habits |
| Direct-booking incentives (perk for booking direct) | Days | Low | Medium — amplifier, but needs a direct channel to point to first |
| One-page website + booking engine + Google connectivity | Weeks–months | Higher | Strategic (phase 2) — unlocks Free Booking Links, but slower and domestic guests still prefer chat |
| Paid metasearch / Hotel Ads | Days | Ongoing ad spend | Low here — mostly feeds OTAs |

**Recommended sequence:**
1. **Now (week 1):** Activate the **WhatsApp chat button on GBP**; confirm the Call button/number is correct and answered. Set up WhatsApp Business with greeting, quick replies, labels, follow-ups.
2. **Weeks 1–2:** Train front-desk on fast, structured responses; start logging inquiry → booking conversion; add a modest **direct-booking perk** (e.g., free late checkout / welcome item for WhatsApp/phone bookings).
3. **Phase 2 (later):** Build a simple one-page site + booking engine to participate in **Free Booking Links** and capture guests who prefer self-service web booking.

**Why:** The hotel already has the hardest asset to build — **digital trust (430 reviews) and Google visibility**. The only missing piece is an obvious, prominent **direct contact path** at the moment of search. WhatsApp + Call on the GBP supplies exactly that, at essentially zero cost, and is the realistic way to claw back 15–25% commissions on a meaningful slice of bookings.
