# Local SEO Playbook — Hotel Barahona 72 (Google Map Pack)

**Goal:** rank higher in the Google Local/Map Pack for "hoteles en barranquilla," "hotel barato barranquilla," "hotel cerca estadio metropolitano," and convert more of the people who see the listing.
**Assets:** Google Business Profile ("Ayenda 1313 Barahona 72") + **~430 Google reviews**, OTA listings. **No website (yet).**

---

## 1. How Google ranks local hotel results (2025–2026)

Google's three pillars: **Relevance, Distance, Prominence.** Industry studies (Whitespark Local Search Ranking Factors 2026, BrightLocal, Sterling Sky, Merchynt) converge on:

- **Google Business Profile signals ≈ 32%** of Local Pack influence (categories, business info, photos, completeness).
- **Review signals ≈ 20%** (up from 16% in 2023) — quantity + **velocity/recency** + rating + **textual content/keywords** + responses.
- **Citations/NAP, on-page (website) signals, links, and behavioral/engagement signals** make up the rest.

What this means for THIS hotel:

- **Distance:** for "cerca estadio" queries, the pin-to-stadium distance is decisive. **Never hide the address** — Sterling Sky's "near me" study found hiding the address correlates *negatively* with ranking. Verify the address and confirm the map pin is exactly on the building.
- **Relevance:** driven by **primary category = "Hotel"** plus relevant secondary categories (e.g., "Budget hotel"/"Hotel económico," "Lodging," "Inn" where Google's Colombia taxonomy allows). BrightLocal found profiles using ~4 extra categories had the best average map rank (5.9). Do **not** keyword-stuff the business name (against Google policy; risks suspension) — put the keywords in **categories, description, attributes, Q&A, and reviews** instead.
- **Prominence:** reviews + links. The 430-review base is a strong prominence asset.
- **Behavioral/engagement** (branded searches, clicks, calls, direction requests) is the hardest-to-measure but, per practitioners, one of the strongest signals. Drive it by sharing the Google Maps link on WhatsApp/social and keeping the profile compelling.

---

## 2. Reviews — the engine (is 430 enough?)

**Yes, 430 is strong** — well past the 50-review (≈30% visibility lift) and 100-review (≈20% higher local-pack rank) thresholds the studies cite. BUT total count is not enough on its own:

- **Recency & velocity beat raw total.** Sterling Sky: reviews earned *this month* correlate more with ranking than lifetime total; businesses that stop getting reviews lose ground to rivals pulling **15–45 new reviews/month**. Fresh reviews (<30 days) can lift rankings ~15% (GatherUp via DesignWorks).
- **Action:** install a steady pipeline — ask every guest at checkout, send a **direct Google review link via WhatsApp/SMS** post-stay. Target **20–30 new reviews/month**. (No incentives, no cherry-picking positives — against Google policy.)
- **Text + keywords in reviews matter.** Reviews with real text outrank rating-only reviews; the words guests use feed relevance. Gently prompt guests to mention what's true: *"si te resultó cómodo para ir al estadio o por el precio, menciónalo."* Aim for reviews naturally containing "hotel económico," "buena ubicación," "cerca del Estadio Metropolitano," "habitaciones limpias."
- **Respond to reviews.** Replying to **80%+** of reviews is associated with a **10–20% ranking lift** (Search Engine Land via DesignWorks) and builds trust. Respond in **Spanish**, within ~48 hrs, newest first; address complaints concretely. With 430 reviews, prioritize recent ones, then work backward.
- **Rating:** keep average above ~4.2 if possible; rating correlates with rank (though it's not the only factor).

## 3. Photos, Posts, Q&A, Attributes

- **Photos** are not a top-tier ranking factor but strongly drive **clicks/calls** (engagement → indirect prominence) and conversion — and hotels are a highly visual vertical. Keep recent, high-res photos of rooms, bathroom, lobby, exterior, breakfast, and stadium/clinic proximity. Refresh **monthly–quarterly**; replace low-quality guest images.
- **Google Posts:** ~**zero direct ranking impact** (practitioner testing), but they keep the profile active and lift click-through. Post **1–2×/month** (or weekly): budget packages, match-day promos (Estadio Metropolitano), Carnaval/event offers, Semana Santa deals.
- **Q&A:** seed it yourself (owners may post + answer). Add Spanish Q&As that mirror target queries: "¿Está cerca del Estadio Metropolitano?", "¿Incluye desayuno?", "¿Hay parqueadero?", "¿Es un hotel económico?" This adds relevance-boosting text.
- **Attributes:** fully populate — Free Wi-Fi, parking, breakfast, air conditioning, family-friendly, budget — these power Google's hotel filters and surface the property to filtered searches.

## 4. The case for a simple one-page website

Even with a strong GBP + OTAs, a basic site helps **both ranking and bookings**:

- **Ranking:** Whitespark lists on-page signals (NAP, keywords in titles/headings, descriptive content) among real local factors. Sterling Sky found more *meaningful* words on the GBP-linked landing page correlate slightly with better local rank. Without a site, Google relies only on the GBP + reviews to infer what the hotel is.
- **Conversions / direct bookings:** a one-pager with a prominent **WhatsApp + phone CTA** captures commission-free inquiries (see ota-and-channels.md). Later, a booking page enables Google **Free Booking Links**.
- **Recommended content** (Spanish, mobile-first, HTTPS, ~600–1,200 words): "Hotel económico en Barranquilla cerca del Estadio Metropolitano," location + approx. distances to stadium/clinics/Puerta de Oro, amenities, exact NAP matching GBP, photo gallery, WhatsApp/phone CTA, links to OTA listings. Avoid keyword-stuffed "novels."
- Once live, set it as the GBP **Website** URL so the Call/Website buttons and citations point to one canonical source.

## 5. NAP consistency & Colombian citations

**NAP = Name, Address, Phone**, identical across every platform. Consistency tells Google the business is real and operational; inconsistency (brand-prefix variations, address formatting, old phone numbers) erodes trust and rankings.

**Citation sources that matter in Colombia:**
- **Hotel/OTA (high authority):** TripAdvisor (most authoritative hotel-specific citation), Booking.com, **Despegar** (major LatAm platform), Expedia/Hotels.com.
- **Colombian directories:** Informa Colombia, PaginasAmarillas.com.co, Cylex, Colombia.com.
- **Social / maps:** Facebook page, **Apple Maps, Waze, Foursquare** (navigation + data syndication).
- **Regulatory:** **Registro Nacional de Turismo (RNT)** — the national tourism registry hotels must hold; an authoritative, quasi-governmental citation. Ensure the registered name/address match the GBP.

**Action:** list every existing listing (GBP, TripAdvisor, Booking, Despegar, Informa, PaginasAmarillas, Facebook, Apple/Waze/Foursquare), make the **Name + Address + Phone identical to the GBP**, fix/merge duplicates and stale "Ayenda" naming variations, and keep an internal NAP master record. Coordinate with the Ayenda brand so chain-level listings don't conflict.

## 6. Top local-SEO actions, ranked by impact (next few weeks)

1. **Fully optimize the GBP** — verify primary category "Hotel" + add relevant secondary categories ("Budget hotel"/económico, Lodging); confirm address + exact map pin (never hidden); complete hours, phone, and all attributes (Wi-Fi, parking, breakfast, budget, family-friendly); write a keyword-natural Spanish description. *Biggest, fastest lever for relevance on "hotel barato" + "cerca estadio."*
2. **Launch a review-velocity engine** — checkout ask + WhatsApp/SMS direct review link; **20–30 new reviews/month**, prompting natural mentions of price + stadium proximity. *Recency/velocity beats the 430 total.*
3. **Respond to reviews in Spanish** — hit 80%+ response rate, newest first, within 48 hrs (10–20% ranking lift + trust).
4. **Add WhatsApp chat button + correct Call button to GBP** — converts ranking/visibility into commission-free bookings (ties to channels strategy).
5. **Audit NAP + citations** across TripAdvisor/Booking/Despegar/Informa/PaginasAmarillas/Facebook/Apple/Waze + confirm RNT registration matches.
6. **Build the simple one-page website** (Spanish, mobile-first), link it from GBP, seed it with stadium/clinic proximity + budget positioning; later add a booking page for Free Booking Links.

**Supporting / ongoing:** seed Q&A, refresh photos monthly–quarterly, post 1–2×/month around events, and track GBP Insights (impressions, calls, direction requests) + Map Pack positions for the three target queries from different devices/locations.
