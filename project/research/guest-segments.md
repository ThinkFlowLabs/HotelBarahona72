# Guest Segments — Hotel Barahona 72 (Calle 72 / barrio Prado, Barranquilla)

**Profile:** mid-budget 3-star, ~USD 35–60/night, mostly domestic Colombian guests, north-central location (Prado), near Estadio Romelio Martínez, within reach of clinics, business corridors and event venues.

Shares below are informed estimates from Barranquilla's economic/medical/event profile + Colombian booking-behavior data; the hotel should refine them against its own PMS/OTA data. They sum to ~100%.

> **Headline:** Business + medical travelers together are ~46% of demand and lean toward DIRECT/WhatsApp booking — the highest-value, most defensible segments. Event, VFR, sports and budget-leisure segments are more OTA-dependent.

## Why this mix (Barranquilla context)

- **Industrial + port + service hub** → steady weekday corporate flow; Prado is central and well-connected to business corridors, justifying its #1 share.
- **Regional health hub** → patients/families from across the Caribbean coast and interior (Del Mar Center / Clínica Iberoamérica explicitly markets health + lodging; metasearch surfaces 100+ hotels near Clínica General del Norte from ~EUR 22). Medical demand is the most year-round, appointment-driven, and least seasonal segment.
- **Carnaval + concerts + Junior + Puerta de Oro** → episodic but intense event/sports peaks (see demand-calendar.md).
- **VFR is a dominant form of Colombian domestic travel** → relatives act as gatekeepers and often phone/WhatsApp the hotel directly.
- **Regional mobility** (Venezuela + Santa Marta/Cartagena/Riohacha) → a price-floor budget-leisure base.

## Booking-channel behavior (Colombian domestic travelers)

- **~81% of bookings are made online** (Booking.com Colombia study, 2018): 42% via travel-agency websites, 39% via apps/OTAs like Booking.com; only **20% by phone or in person**. Skews even more digital since.
- **Top decision criteria:** facilities/infrastructure (39%), transport convenience (36%), other travelers' comments/reviews (32%) → reviews and location are decisive, especially in the budget tier.
- **WhatsApp is central** to Colombian commerce. Hotel CRM data (Journeytok): WhatsApp message **open rates ~95%** vs email ~20–25% (in-stay email ~10% vs WhatsApp 95–100%). Colombian social content actively promotes "Reserva por WhatsApp." Caveat: warn guests about WhatsApp impersonation scams (Booking.com never asks for data via WhatsApp) and use a clearly branded official number.

**Channel rule of thumb by segment:**
- **OTA-dominant:** event attendees, sports fans, regional budget leisure (first-time, price-shopping).
- **Direct/WhatsApp-leaning:** business (repeat), medical, VFR, government (need customized arrangements, invoicing, or trust via conversation).

## SEO/keyword takeaways

Colombian hotel queries follow: **[hotel type] + [price/quality adjective] + [city/barrio] + [proximity/purpose]**.

- **Price intent:** "barato," "económico," "desde 100.000 pesos."
- **Proximity intent:** "cerca del estadio," "cerca de Clínica X," "en El Prado," "cerca de Puerta de Oro."
- **Purpose intent:** "empresarial," "para familias," "para pacientes," "para funcionarios."
- **Brand intent:** "Ayenda hoteles en Barranquilla," "Booking Barranquilla hoteles."
- **WhatsApp intent (growing):** "hotel en Barranquilla reservas WhatsApp."

Highest-leverage angles for THIS hotel: **business ("empresarial" + factura)**, **medical ("cerca de clínica" + tranquilo)**, and **stadium/Carnaval proximity** — these match both the location and the segments that convert to direct bookings.
