# Barranquilla Hotel Demand Calendar (SEO + Google Ads planning)

**Client:** Hotel Barahona 72 (Ayenda 1313 Barahona 72), Calle 72, barrio Prado, Barranquilla
**Purpose:** Map the month-by-month events that drive hotel demand, separate SEARCH windows from STAY windows, and time SEO content + Google Ads accordingly.

---

## 1. Carnaval de Barranquilla — the #1 dated demand spike

Carnaval is anchored to the Catholic calendar: the four core days run Saturday (Batalla de Flores) through Martes de Carnaval, ending the day before Ash Wednesday.

| Year | Ash Wednesday | Core Carnaval days (Sat–Tue) |
|------|---------------|------------------------------|
| **2026** | Wed 18 Feb 2026 | **Sat 14 – Tue 17 Feb 2026** |
| **2027** | Wed 10 Feb 2027 | **Sat 6 – Tue 9 Feb 2027** (officially confirmed) |

The 2027 dates (6–9 Feb 2027) are already officially announced by the Carnaval organization ("El Carnaval de Barranquilla 2027 ya tiene fechas"), so ad copy can name the dates safely now.

**Impact on hotels:**
- Citywide occupancy routinely exceeds **90%**, with Cotelco (Colombian hotel association) reporting multiple hotels hitting **100%** during the core days.
- Rates for the Saturday/Sunday of Carnaval typically run **50%–150% above** baseline February rates. Kayak data shows average Barranquilla hotel prices jump from ~USD 42 (October) to ~USD 130 (February), confirming February as the single highest-ADR month.
- Saturday (Batalla de Flores) and Sunday are the absolute peak; Friday and Monday are softer but still far above baseline.
- Barrio Prado is a recognized hotel district and is marketed by neighbors (Hotel El Prado, etc.) as a Carnaval base, so a Prado hotel captures spillover when upscale properties sell out.

**Booking window (search vs stay):**
- Domestic Colombians (Bogotá, Medellín, Cali, Bucaramanga) typically book **30–90 days out**; international/regional visitors **60–120 days out** (they must also secure flights).
- Central/well-located 3-star hotels often **sell out the Sat/Sun 2–4 weeks before** the event; budget hotels farther from parade routes may hold rooms until ~1 week out but still run well above normal occupancy.
- **SEARCH starts the moment dates are announced** (a year+ ahead) and intensifies sharply in the **6 weeks before** the event.

**Marketing implication:** Publish/refresh the Carnaval landing content by **mid-2026** for the 2027 edition (so it indexes), then ramp Google Ads from ~4 months out, peaking 1–2 months before. Turn off ads for sold-out nights and redirect spend to shoulder nights (Thu before / Wed after).

## 2. Football — Atlético Junior + Colombia national team (Estadio Metropolitano)

Estadio Metropolitano Roberto Meléndez (capacity ~46,692 domestic / ~49,000 for World Cup qualifiers) is in southern Barranquilla, ~20–30 min by road from Prado.

> Note: The hotel sits next to **Estadio Romelio Martínez** (a smaller stadium near Calle 72), but the large crowds come to the **Metropolitano**. Both matter for "hotel cerca del estadio" intent.

**Atlético Junior home matches:**
- Liga BetPlay runs two tournaments/year (Apertura + Clausura), ~19 regular-season matches each = **~9–10 home games per tournament, ~18–20 home league games/year**.
- Add playoffs + Copa Colombia + continental cups (Copa Sudamericana/Libertadores) and an active season reaches **20–30 home matches/year**.
- Example fixtures confirm weekday + weekend scheduling: vs Unión Magdalena (Wed 23 Jul 2025), vs Boyacá Chicó (Sat 2 Aug 2025), vs Bucaramanga (Mon 18 Aug 2025); 2026: vs Bucaramanga (24 Mar), vs Cali (3 Apr), vs Llaneros (19 Apr).

**Do visiting fans book hotels?** Yes, for high-profile weekend matches. Colombian football has strong traveling-supporter culture; matches against **América de Cali, Millonarios, Atlético Nacional** draw away fans who arrive Friday, depart Sunday, and favor budget/midscale hotels. Weekday and mid-table matches generate smaller, mostly local crowds (less hotel demand). Treat **weekend matches vs big rivals as secondary demand peaks**.

**Colombia national team home World Cup qualifiers:** Played at the Metropolitano (e.g., Colombia vs Peru, 6 Jun). A qualification cycle has **~8–9 home qualifiers**, drawing fans nationwide + media + sponsors. These can lift occupancy **20–50 points** and ADR **20–80%** on match days, second only to Carnaval. Note: the national team's continued use of the Metropolitano has been "under study," so confirm each cycle.

**Search window for matches:** Short — fans book **2–8 weeks out**, spiking at ticket-release and again as match day nears. Use evergreen "hotel cerca del estadio" content year-round + match-timed Google Ads for marquee fixtures.

## 3. Business, conventions & cultural festivals (Puerta de Oro)

Puerta de Oro Centro de Eventos del Caribe is the main convention center. Recurring demand drivers:

| Event | Typical timing | Notes |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| **Sabor Barranquilla** (gastronomy festival) | **Late August** (Thu/Fri–Sun) | Consistent August slot; multi-night stays; foodies + trade |
| **Barranquijazz** (jazz festival) | **Early September** | Multi-venue, multi-day; cultural tourists; lifts an otherwise quiet period |
| Trade fairs / congresses (construction, energy, logistics, health) | **Mar–Apr and Sep–Oct** | Mid-week (Tue–Thu) demand, 3–5 night stays, predictable long lead times |

Business/convention demand is valuable because it fills **mid-week** troughs that leisure peaks miss, and attendees are exactly the budget-conscious 3-star buyer. Target "hotel para eventos/congresos en Barranquilla" and "hotel cerca de Puerta de Oro."

## 4. FIFA World Cup 2026 (Jun–Jul 2026) — minimal direct spillover

The 2026 World Cup is hosted by **USA/Mexico/Canada**; **Colombia is not a host**, so there is **no match-driven demand** in Barranquilla. Spillover is thematic only:
- Some fans choose to experience the tournament in Barranquilla (national-team-city identity); one hotel (bh Barranquilla) marketed a guest staying 30 days to watch every Colombia match.
- Realistic upside = **watch parties, F&B revenue, and occasional long-stay/regional fans**, not structural occupancy. Treat as a content/brand opportunity ("vivir el Mundial en Barranquilla"), not a budget driver.

## 5. Seasonality: high vs low season + Semana Santa

**High season (highest occupancy + ADR):**
- **February** — Carnaval; the single highest month (Kayak avg ~USD 130).
- **December–January** — Christmas/New Year, returning diaspora, regional leisure.
- **Mid-year (June–July)** — school vacations + possible national-team matches.
- **Semana Santa (March/April, movable)** — Barranquilla recorded **352,000+ visitors** one recent Semana Santa (Gran Malecón etc.); strong VFR + religious + leisure demand.

**Low / shoulder season (discount + promo months):**
- **April–May** (post-Carnaval/Easter lull) and parts of **September–October** (outside festivals/congresses). Kayak shows **October (~USD 42) and December among the lowest average rates**; ADR can fall 20–40% vs high season.

**Weekday pattern:** Kayak data — **Wednesday cheapest (~USD 62 avg), Saturday highest (~USD 95)**. Business/medical/government guests anchor mid-week occupancy; leisure/event/sports anchor weekends.

## 6. SEARCH vs STAY — the timing rule

| Demand driver | When they SEARCH | When they STAY |
|---------------|------------------|----------------|
| Carnaval 2027 | From announcement (a year out); peak 6 wks before | 6–9 Feb 2027 |
| National-team qualifier | 2–8 wks before (ticket release) | Match day ± 1 night |
| Junior weekend match vs rival | 2–6 wks before | Fri–Sun of match |
| Conventions (Puerta de Oro) | Months ahead (corporate approval) | Tue–Thu of event |
| Sabor Barranquilla / Barranquijazz | 1–2 months before | Late Aug / early Sep |
| Semana Santa | 4–8 wks before | Holy Week |
| Low-season leisure | Days to 2 wks before (last-minute) | Apr–May, Sep–Oct |

**Rule:** SEO content must be live and indexed **well before** the search window opens (front-load); Google Ads budget should ramp with proximity to the stay date and shut off once sold out.

## Quick-reference demand map (month by month)

- **Jan** — High (holiday tail + Carnaval search ramp). Carnaval pre-events.
- **Feb** — PEAK (Carnaval, 6–9 Feb in 2027). Highest ADR of the year.
- **Mar** — Medium; congresses begin; some Junior matches; Semana Santa search ramp.
- **Apr** — Semana Santa peak (if April) then shoulder/lull begins.
- **May** — Low/shoulder. Promote discounts.
- **Jun** — Mid-year up-tick (school holidays + possible qualifier).
- **Jul** — Mid-year leisure + Junior Clausura home games.
- **Aug** — Up (Sabor Barranquilla late Aug + Junior fixtures).
- **Sep** — Up early (Barranquijazz) then mid; congresses.
- **Oct** — Lowest ADR; lean on business/medical + promos.
- **Nov** — Medium; congresses; Junior playoff push.
- **Dec** — High (Christmas/New Year).
